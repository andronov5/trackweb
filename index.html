New athletes and families

Start here if you are joining, transferring, or just curious.

Track and field has room for many kinds of athletes: sprinters, distance runners, jumpers, throwers, hurdlers, relay runners, and students still figuring out where they fit. This page is designed to help you understand the program before the first practice or meet.

What you can expect

Practices are built around effort, consistency, improvement, and team culture. Some athletes arrive with years of club or middle-school experience. Others try track for the first time in high school. Coaches help athletes find events that match their strengths while also building confidence, fitness, and race-day habits.

Meet-day guide

What athletes and families should know before competition day.

Track meets can feel confusing at first because many events happen across the same afternoon. The basics below help everyone arrive prepared and support the team well.

Team culture

Simple habits that make the whole team better.

Track is both individual and team-based. Your marks matter, but so do your consistency, communication, sportsmanship, and willingness to support teammates in every event area.
